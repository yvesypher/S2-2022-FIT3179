120 years of Nobel Prize

(1901 - 2021)

Established in 1901, Nobel Prize is a prestigious annual award for outstanding achievement in such fields as Physics, Chemistry, Physiology or Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Science. The prize was founded on the will of Alfred Nobel which states that bequeathed all of his "remaining realisable assets" to establish prizes awarded to "those who, during the preceding year, have conferred the greatest benefit to humankind". Nobel Prizes were first awarded in 1901 for five categories except for Economic Science. Not until 1968 was this category introduced and awarded.

Alfred Nobel was a Swedish chemist, engineer, and industrialist most famously known for the invention of dynamite. Despite bring him wealth, his inventions during that time made war more devastating. As a pacifist, he attempts to leave a better legacy in the world than having improved military weaponry through his ingenuity. That was why he devoted his assets to awarding anyone with enormous contributions to the world. Only few days left until the 2022 Nobel prize ceremony, let's go through interesting facts and statistics about this reputable prize.

Nobel prize can be shared by more than 1 laureate

Click on legend to filter a category / Hold "Shift" key and click to filter multiple categories

A Nobel prize can be shared up to three individuals. However, Nobel Peace Prize can be awarded to organisations of more than three people. Nobel Prizes are not awarded posthumously. In case a person is awarded but dies before receiving it, the prize is still presented. It is observable that Physiology or Medicine category has the highest number of Nobel laureates overall and shared by three laureates. Meanwhile, Literature category has the most individual laureate and no three-shared prize awardees.

Nobel prize can make a laureate a millionaire

Click on legend to filter a category / Hold "Shift" key and click to filter multiple categories

Money prize awarded to Nobel laureates are taken from the Nobel Foundation. This fund was established by Alfred Nobel's fortune, which was 31 million SEK (approximately US$ 280 million in 2021). Owing to the exemption from all taxes in Sweden since 1946 and from investment taxes in USA since 1953, the foundation's investments have become more profitable. As of 31/12/2007, the fund amounted to 3.628 billion SEK (approximately US$600 million in 2021).
The amount of prize money depends on how much money the foundation can award each year. Moreover, money prize is divided if there is prize share. If two laureates share the prize in a category, the award grant is divided equally between the recipients. If there are three, the awarding committee has the option of dividing the grant equally, or awarding one-half to one recipient and one-quarter to each of the others.
